## Motivation

After Cycle-Balance was downgraded to a positive-path baseline, the next pilot
tested a different mechanism family: depth / oversmoothing-aware training.
DDC asks whether nodes whose shallow and deep encoder states disagree should be
weighted differently or receive an auxiliary shallow-depth contrastive target.

Recent novelty risk is nontrivial:

- BlockGCL / *Oversmoothing: A Nightmare for Graph Contrastive Learning?*
  already frames oversmoothing as a GCL training problem:
  https://arxiv.org/abs/2306.02117
- Stage-aware GCL / SAGCL style work already uses stage-level contrastive
  alignment in 2026:
  https://ojs.aaai.org/index.php/AAAI/article/view/38695/42657

Therefore DDC needed a strong empirical signal before deeper refinement.

## Implementation

Added a `DepthTraceGCN` encoder for DDC variants. It exposes shallow and deep
states while preserving the existing BGRL evaluation protocol.

Variants:

- `ddc_control`: same DDC trace encoder, standard BGRL loss.
- `ddc_weight`: reweights node-level BGRL losses by shallow/deep
  representation disagreement.
- `ddc_aux`: adds a weighted auxiliary alignment from deep predictions to
  the other view's shallow state.

DDC metrics logged:

- `ddc_disagreement_mean`
- `ddc_disagreement_std`
- `ddc_aux_loss_mean`

## Interpretation

DDC is stable and measurable, but it is not a promising main route:

- Cora gets a small same-architecture gain, but the DDC encoder architecture is
  weaker than the standard BGRL control and far below LIFT-Portfolio.
- CiteSeer has almost no gain.
- Chameleon, the critical heterophily dataset where current baselines matter
  most, regresses versus the DDC control and is far below both BGRL and
  Positive-Path LIFT.

## Decision

`FAILED_SPLIT0_GATE`.

Do not run WebKB, 10-split, external baselines, or a DDC paper refinement. Keep
the implementation as a diagnostic for depth-disagreement weighting, but restart
the main-method search with a different mechanism family.
