# Final Proposal Draft: LIFT-PROP-GCL

**Date**: 2026-06-22 12:50  
**Status**: `STRONG_BASELINE_LOW_NOVELTY`, not paper-ready  
**Candidate**: LIFT-PROP / LIFT-Portfolio, Label-free edge-LIFT gated propagation controls

## Problem Anchor

Recent graph contrastive learning work shows that simple propagation can rival
or beat complex learned GCL objectives. The unresolved practical problem is not
only how to design stronger propagation, but how to decide when propagation
should be used at all. In the current experiments, propagation is strongly
helpful on Chameleon/Squirrel and harmful on WebKB/Actor-style raw-dominant
graphs.

## Method Thesis

LIFT-PROP-GCL uses a label-free reliability statistic to route between raw
features and propagated features before contrastive training or evaluation. It
keeps the contribution narrow:

> edge-vs-random cosine lift predicts whether propagation creates useful
> graph-aligned structure or merely oversmooths/damages raw-feature signal.

## Selector v1

Let `P` be the normalized adjacency with self-loops and let `X_k = P^k X`.
Define:

```text
lift(X_k) = mean_(i,j in E) cos(X_k[i], X_k[j])
          - mean_(u,v random) cos(X_k[u], X_k[v])
```

Selector v1:

```text
if lift(X_2) < 0.35:
    select K = 0
else if lift(X_2) <= lift(X_1) + 0.02:
    select K = 1
else:
    select K = 2
```

The selected representation is `X_K`. In the GCL framing, this is the
reliability gate for a propagation-contrastive view: propagation is enabled
only when the graph produces nontrivial edge-lift over random-pair alignment.

## Current Evidence

Selector v1 matches the observed oracle K on 7/8 current datasets/settings:

| Dataset / setting | Selected K | Result | Oracle K | Status |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---|
| Cora split-0 | 2 | 0.826488 | 3 | near miss |
| CiteSeer split-0 | 2 | 0.709617 | 2 | hit |
| Chameleon 10-split | 2 | 0.685746 +/- 0.021187 | 2 | hit |
| Texas 10-split | 0 | 0.829730 +/- 0.051042 | 0 | hit |
| Wisconsin 10-split | 0 | 0.839216 +/- 0.043157 | 0 | hit |
| Cornell 10-split | 0 | 0.816216 +/- 0.059487 | 0 | hit |
| Actor 10-split fast grid | 0 | 0.347566 | 0 | hit |
| Squirrel 10-split fast grid | 1 | 0.520365 | 1 | hit |

Novelty check score is `5.5/10`, `PROCEED_WITH_CAUTION`. The closest prior is
PROPGCL. The main differentiator must be raw-vs-propagation abstention and
label-free K selection, not propagation itself.

PROPGCL-facing first-pass comparison is positive for the selector claim:
LIFT-PROP v1 reaches mean oracle gap `0.001096` across the eight observed
settings, while the PROPGCL Appendix-E reported-step heuristic has mean oracle
gap `0.011052`. This does **not** beat learned PROPGCL (`PROP-GRACE` /
`PROP-DGI`); it only establishes a label-free selector delta over fixed
PROP-step heuristics.

R077/R078 attempted to turn LIFT-PROP into a fuller GCL method and failed.
Edge-NCE learned propagation coefficients improved only Cora but damaged
CiteSeer and Chameleon; node-wise LIFT routing improved only Cora and hurt
Chameleon/Texas/Wisconsin. The useful contribution is therefore the global
selector itself, not learned coefficient training or per-node K routing.

R079-R081 then tested LIFT-Stack: if global LIFT selects raw, use raw; otherwise
concatenate normalized `[X, PX, P2X, P3X]`. This is empirically stronger than
single-depth LIFT on Cora, CiteSeer, Chameleon, and Squirrel while preserving
raw-dominant Texas/Wisconsin/Cornell/Actor. However, the mechanism overlaps
with SIGN, SGC, Fixed Aggregation Features, and PROPGCL-style propagation.

R082-R087 tested compact bridges from LIFT-Stack to a fuller learned GCL
method. LIFT-HC-GCL trains a hop-drop contrastive MLP over the multi-hop stack;
it improves Cora/Wisconsin locally but regresses on CiteSeer and Chameleon.
Concatenating existing BGRL/GDC/TD checkpoint embeddings with LIFT-Stack also
fails the complementarity gate: Cora improves, but CiteSeer, Chameleon, Texas,
and Wisconsin regress. Channel-wise edge-lift weighting over LIFT-Stack finds a
Chameleon split-0 local gain but fails robustness on Chameleon/Squirrel
10-split. Therefore, neither generic contrastive transformation, direct
learned-checkpoint residual concatenation, simple channel gating, nor fixed
raw-propagation interaction blocks are viable main-method paths.

R088 corrects the required fixed-propagation control. Chameleon full-grid
prefers single P2/global LIFT (`0.685746` over 10 splits) over LIFT-Stack
(`0.671053`). Cora/CiteSeer/Squirrel still prefer stack, and
WebKB/Actor/Cornell prefer raw. The required control is now LIFT-Portfolio:

```text
if selected K = 0: raw
elif selected K = 2 and lift(X) < 0.05: P2X
else: [X, PX, P2X, P3X]
```

R089/R090 tested EPI-BGRL, a GPU-trained mechanism outside the fixed LIFT
family. It partitions nodes into label-free environments by degree and
raw-feature neighborhood drift, then balances BGRL node losses across
environments. This does not rescue the main-method search. EPI improves Cora
over same-run BGRL (`0.840794` vs `0.833872`) but remains below
LIFT-Portfolio split-0 (`0.842640`), regresses on CiteSeer and Chameleon, and
ties weak BGRL controls on Texas/Wisconsin while remaining far below raw/LIFT.
EPI is therefore negative evidence, not a route for 10-split expansion.

R091/R092 tested PAB-BGRL, a prealignment-abstention variant that downweights
nodes whose BGRL positive alignment is likely already trivialized by message
passing. It produces a small BGRL-internal signal on CiteSeer, Chameleon, and
Texas, but it is still far below LIFT-Portfolio/raw controls and has high
novelty risk near SPGCL's 2026 pre-alignment analysis. PAB is therefore also
not a main-method route.

R093/R094 tested CCR-GCL, a learned residual branch trained to be orthogonal to
a fixed LIFT-Portfolio teacher. This gives the strongest learned-complement
signal so far on Cora (`0.842640 -> 0.855099`), but the same residual and
certificate damage CiteSeer, Chameleon, Texas, and Wisconsin. The decisive
failure is not collapse; it is label-free safety. Residual edge-lift and
teacher-anchor orthogonality do not reliably predict whether adding the learned
branch will help node classification.

R095 reframed this as CCR-SAFE, a residual-safety diagnostic over the existing
CCR checkpoints. The best current rule, `stack_moderate_residual`, uses the
learned residual only when LIFT-Portfolio selected the multi-hop stack, the
residual edge-lift is at least `0.2`, and the residual edge cosine is at most
`0.8`. On split 0 it accepts only Cora (`0.842640 -> 0.851869`) and rejects
CiteSeer, Chameleon, Texas, and Wisconsin, avoiding the old certificate's
damage. This is promising but explicitly not paper-ready because the threshold
rule was selected on the same diagnostic batch; it must survive fresh splits
unchanged before becoming an active main-method candidate.

R096 froze those thresholds and tested fresh checkpoints. The gate fails:
Cora split 1 drops from `0.844024` to `0.832949`, and Cora split 2 drops from
`0.854638` to `0.850023` because the safety rule still accepts the residual.
CiteSeer split 1 is protected, but that is insufficient. CCR-SAFE is therefore
`FAILED_FRESH_SPLIT_GATE`, not a live method proposal.

R097 then tried a different causal-training-path idea, SBB-BGRL. Instead of
removing edges, SBB keeps low raw-cosine neighbor messages in the forward pass
but stop-gradients those message paths. The result is negative: Cora
(`0.832949 -> 0.815413`), CiteSeer (`0.692712 -> 0.688204`), and Chameleon
(`0.438596 -> 0.427632`) regress. Texas/Wisconsin improve only relative to
weak BGRL controls and remain far below LIFT-Portfolio/raw. SBB is not a live
proposal.

R098 tested a fixed view-set dispersion proxy before implementing any
uncertainty/conformal GCL objective. Adding propagation-view mean/std/statistics
to LIFT-Portfolio gives only a tiny Cora gain (`0.842640 -> 0.844947`) and
hurts CiteSeer and Chameleon (`0.725770 -> 0.720135`,
`0.699561 -> 0.662281`). Since UGCL already covers uncertainty-guided graph
contrastive learning at a broader level, this proxy has neither empirical nor
novelty support.

R099 tested a low-rank bottleneck proxy over LIFT-Portfolio before spending GPU
time on a learned low-rank GCL objective. The only positive is local to
CiteSeer at rank 32 (`0.725770 -> 0.731781`). Cora has no improvement
(`0.842640 -> 0.842178` at the best tested rank), and Chameleon loses a large
amount of accuracy (`0.699561 -> 0.651316`). This looks like dataset-specific
linear-probe regularization, not a robust representation-learning mechanism.
The novelty case is also weak because low-rank graph contrastive learning is
already a nearby direction.

## Required Positioning

Do not claim:

- first use of propagation in GCL;
- first feature-node graph construction;
- first graph/raw dual-view method;
- first adaptive spectral or filtering GCL method.

Claim only if supported:

- a simple zero-label edge-lift statistic predicts when graph propagation helps
  versus hurts;
- reliability-gated propagation protects raw-dominant heterophily datasets
  while retaining Chameleon/Squirrel gains;
- the gate complements PROPGCL-style propagation by removing the need for
  validation-label propagation-depth selection in the raw-vs-propagation
  decision.

## Immediate Scope

Keep `evaluate_lift_prop.py`, `reproduce_lift_prop.py`, and
`evaluate_lift_stack.py` / `evaluate_lift_portfolio.py` as formal training-free
controls. Do not claim LIFT-Portfolio as the final main method without a deeper
novelty argument against
SIGN/FAF/PROPGCL. Also do not promote LIFT-HC-GCL, checkpoint-residual
concatenation, LIFT Channel Gate, fixed LIFT interaction features, EPI-BGRL, or
PAB-BGRL. Do not promote CCR-GCL unless a stronger label-free residual safety
criterion is validated on fresh splits; R096 shows the current CCR-SAFE rule
does not meet this bar. Also do not promote SBB-style low-cosine message
stop-gradient, fixed LIFT view-set dispersion, or low-rank compression over
LIFT-Portfolio. The next main-method search should restart with a different
mechanism family, using LIFT-Portfolio as the required control to beat.
